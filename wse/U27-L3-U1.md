BALTIMORE-WASHINGTON INTERNATIONAL(BWI) AIRPORT HISTORY AT A GLANCE
1784
Edward Warren, at the age of 13, make first recorded manned flight in the United States, in a ballon, over the City of Balitimore.
1906
College Park Airport beings operations. Today, it is the oldest operating airport in the United States.
1946
Master plan for a new airport in Maryland complete. The Baltimore Aviation Commission selects 3,200-acrc site 10 miles south of Baltimore and 30 miles north of Washington, D.C.
1947
Construction on new airport begins and is officially named Friendship International Airport.
1950
President Harry S. Truman delicates Friendship International Airport on June 24th. The airport is viewed as the most advanced in the United States.
1957
Friendship International Airport is the East Coast terminus of the record-breaking transcontinental flight by first Boaeing 707 jetliner.
1973
The airport is renamed Baltimore-Washington International Airport.(BWI).
1980
BWI Amtrak Rail Station opens, serving passengers throughout the Northeast Corridor. BWI becomes first airport in the U.S. to have a rail station on airport grounds.
1989
BWI breaks all previous passenger traffic records, reaching more than one million passengers in a month for the first time(Aug). Totals 10.4 million passengers for the year.
1999
BWI hiker/biker trail complete. The 12.5-mile recreational trail is the only one of its kind in the country that encircles the property of a major commercial airport.
2001
BWI named the fastest growing airport amone the 30 busiest in North America. BWI is credited with being one of the most powerful economic engines in Maryland, generating $6.5 billion and 85,000 jobs for the state's economy.
More than 20.4 million passengers choose the "easy come, easy go" airport, new record for the eighth consecutive year.
2004
Free Arrival Waiting Area, also know as the Cell Phone Lot opens at BWI. The lot provides drivers with about 50 short-term parking spaces as they await cell phone contact from arriving travelers. The lot is an alternative that saves drivers time, while limiting traffic in front of the Airport terminal druing construction.
Tripling the number of parking spaces avaliable close to the main terminal building, BWI opened the new Daily Garage. The garage offers 8,400 spaces and was constructed with "Smart Park" technology. "Smart Park" is an automated guidance system intended to make finding a space quick and easy. At the time of openning, BWI was the only East Coast airport to mount ultrasonic sensors over each parking space to monitor the availability of spaces.
2006
BWI ranked among the best North American Airports for Passenger Satisfaction. The J.D Power and Associates and Aviation Week study ranked BWI Thurgood Marshall airport third in overall passenger satisfacton among mediu-size(10 million to 30 million passengers) airports. The study measured overall airport satisfaction base on eight specific performance factors - airport accessibility, check-in/baggage check, terminal facilities, food and beverage, retail services, baggage claim, and immigration/customs control.
